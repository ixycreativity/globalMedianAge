The concept & how to understand it:

Median age is the age that divides a population into two numerically equally sized groups - that is, half the people are younger than this age and half are older.

I represented the population as 100 people divided in two groups: 50/50 people. Between them, there is a red line, and at the bottom, there is an age axis. The red line is positioned at the median age that you are viewing.

Countries show as flags. It doesn't matter where they are positioned - to the left or to the right (for design reasons I've put them always on the expanded half). However, the countries that show at once are ordered from top to bottom, equal median ages are placed on the same row.

The axis' smallest division is half a year - this is what you get into view. If I was to show by year there would have been too many countries at once into view; if I was to show them by decimal values - like the data- there would have been too small divisions on the ruler to be explored.

On the axis, there are white marks which show where is data.

Notes:

For the elder part, I've used the fixed age of 75 years which is close to the global life expectancy (72.6 years), however, countries have very different values, therefore the density of the people is not correct, it is rather a visual interpretation of what median age is, rather than a research result.

Also, I've noticed at least 2 countries missing from the data: South Korea and Vatican, there may be others. Besides countries, there is also data for the "World" and the "EU" (European Union) you just have to explore it and discover it.

This data visualization is made for laptop screens, not small screens neither huge ones. It may not work on touch devices.
